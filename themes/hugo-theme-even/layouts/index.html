“With the truth, all given facts harmonize; but with what is false, the truth soon hits a wrong note.”
― Aristotle, The Nicomachean Ethics

Doctoral Researcher | Quantitative Reasoning Fellow | CUNY Graduate Center

Why is it wrong to kiss your sister, even though no one gets hurt? Be relieved; that is a rhetorical question. While philosophers have debated what acts ought to be condemned, my work has explored why people condemn them - especially when no one gets hurt. Beyond trying to understand the various shades of immorality, my more recent work seeks to uncover the structure of the moral domain, and how that structure influences which acts or issues become morally relevant.

Quantitative Support at the SLU Learning Center

In Summer 2020, I have been carrying out an appointment as a Quantitative Reasoning Fellow at the CUNY School of Labor and Urban Studies Learning Center. My role at the Learning Center focuses on building an academic support system for students and faculty, particularly with the aim of facilitating quantitative pedagogy through one-on-one student support, the design and administration of quantitative support workshops, and in-class visitation and tutorials.

During my first year, I provided over 80 hours of one-on-one consultation to students working on quantitative (and qualitative!) undergraduate and graduate capstone research projects and coursework. I also developed and administered workshops guiding students on different aspects of the empirical research process, and am currently developing still others that will be administered in the coming year.

Courses Taught

Brooklyn College has been been a leader in diversity, being formally named the Most Diverse Campus in the North Region and having the #1 diversity index of any campus across all four regions (North, South, Midwest, and West). It also ranks #6 in upward social mobility and plays a crucial role in advancing socioeconomic status of New York City’s minority students.

Although Brooklyn College is a minority-serving institution, it is not yet formally recognized as a Hispanic-serving institution. However, it is projected to become eligible soon (current enrollment is 24.3% Hispanic). It is currently eligible to become an Asian American and Pacific Islander Serving Institution, though to my knowledge does not have the formal designation yet.

Needless to say, Brooklyn College is an interesting and rewarding place to teach!

Summer 2021

Workshop Leader, Statistical Programming in R, Brooklyn College Science Undergraduate Research Gateway Experience (SURGE)

SURGE is an NIH-funded academic support program directed toward incoming Brooklyn College freshman from underrepresented backgrounds. The program is meant to foster interest in pursing MD-PhDs in the social and biomedical sciences while developing vital skills that will make the students competitive applicants. My roll is to lead 45 hours of instruction in statistical programming in R, which provides core knowledge of the R programming language, practice in the a tidy approach to data manipulation, and application of the statistical knowledge that gained through other aspects of the workshop.
